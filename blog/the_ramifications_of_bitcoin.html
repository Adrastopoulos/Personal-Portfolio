The Ramifications of Bitcoin

Context

Bitcoin first achieved parity with the U.S. dollar in 2011. Now, it fluctuates by thousands of dollars every day.
Now, almost everyone with a digital bank participates in a cryptocurrency trade, crossing their fingers in hopes of striking it rich in the next bull run.

The Dark Side of the World's Largest Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin operates on the block-chain, meaning that a complex network of networks must operate constantly to verify transactions against the block-chain. This, along with the high volume of miners, demands a high toll on both energy and hardware industries. (source)

Justifications

The takeaway very much depends on how you feel about Bitcoin. Is it no more than a device for money-laundering, or is it a legitimate decentralized currency, free from government regulation and artificial inflation?
